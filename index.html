Zihan Wang

I am a second-year Ph.D. student in the Department of Mathematics at the Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences, New York University, advised by Qi Lei. My current research interests center on post-training for large language models, especially supervised fine-tuning (SFT) and reinforcement learning (RL). I also study chain-of-thought and reasoning in language models. More broadly, I am interested in optimization theory and AI for mathematics.

Selected Publications

*: equal contribution.

When does Chain-of-Thought Help: A Markovian Perspective

Zihan Wang, Yijun Dong, Qi Lei

arXiv, 2026

Identifies transition alignment as a key determinant of when chain-of-thought prompting improves sample efficiency, and validates the theory with controlled synthetic benchmarks.

arXiv paper

Data Reconstruction Attacks and Defenses: A Systematic Evaluation

Sheng Liu*, Zihan Wang*, Yuxiao Chen, Qi Lei

AISTATS, 2025

Recasts data reconstruction as an inverse problem, derives matching reconstruction-error bounds for two-layer networks, and proposes a utility-matched evaluation protocol.

PMLR arXiv paper

Implicit bias of SGD in L2-regularized linear DNNs: One-way jumps from high to low rank

Zihan Wang, Arthur Jacot

ICLR, 2024 Spotlight

Shows how stochastic gradient dynamics can move from higher-rank minima to lower-rank minima in regularized deep linear networks, while reverse jumps have probability zero.

OpenReview arXiv paper

Reconstructing Training Data from Model Gradient, Provably

Zihan Wang, Jason D. Lee, Qi Lei

AISTATS, 2023

Proves that a single model-gradient query at random initialization can identify training samples under broad neural network assumptions, with an efficient tensor-decomposition reconstruction algorithm.

PMLR arXiv paper
